## Scoring rubric draft (0-100)

This draft rubric is designed for **responsible purchase matching** (helping buyers find breeders aligned with their priorities) while minimizing harm from overconfident labeling. It assumes you display **two outputs**:

* **Breeder Quality Score (0ÔÇô100)** = weighted score based on evidence-backed practices.
* **Evidence Confidence** (High/Medium/Low) = how much of the score is supported by Tier 2/3 evidence rather than self-report.

This is important because some systems explicitly operate on self-reporting (e.g., Good Dog notes health testing levels are based on reported practices and also describes limits to verification), and because registries may not publish all abnormal results unless disclosed/required (CHIC vs non-CHIC). ţłÇciteţłéturn5view1ţłéturn12view2ţłéturn12view0ţłü

### Suggested point-level rules

To keep explanations transparent, each category should be scoreable with **three to five discrete levels** tied to evidence:

* **0% of category points**: not provided / contradictory / red-flagged misinformation.
* **~40%**: self-report only, vague, or incomplete.
* **~70%**: specific details provided + at least one Tier 2 artifact (upload, vet letter, contract excerpt).
* **100%**: Tier 3 verification where applicable (CHIC/OFA links, program confirmation) + clear dates + consistency across claims.

This aligns with real-world constraints: CHIC and OFA provide strong verification paths when results are publicly available; meanwhile, Good Dog explicitly notes limits to verification in its health testing system, which argues for a separate confidence measure. ţłÇciteţłéturn12view0ţłéturn12view2ţłéturn5view1ţłü

## Risk/mitigation section

Ranking or labeling breeders is high-stakes because it can create **false assurance for buyers**, **economic harm for sellers**, and **legal/ethical exposure for the platform**. Multiple major registries/listing services publish explicit disclaimers that they do not guarantee breeder claims or puppy qualityÔÇöUKC states it does not endorse or guarantee breeders and accepts no responsibility for listing information, and the Royal Kennel Club similarly disclaims warranty/fitness and states it cannot guarantee accuracy or vet advertisers. ţłÇciteţłéturn10view0ţłéturn18view0ţłü
